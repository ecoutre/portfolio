I just completed my final year at the University of Southern California. It's been an incredibly fast unbelievable 4 years, and I'm really glad I found what I love to do, learning a lot about myself along the way
I matriculated having many different interests and not a clear direction. I decided to study Computer Science and Business Administration, one of the only offered majors of this type in the nation. I think growing up in Palo Alto, the center of all the magic of Silicon Valley, played a large role in my decision to go down this route. However, I was always looking for a way to combine my artistic and creative talents (see Paintings & Drawings . This is when I realized my calling was to work on product and to design awesome user experiences.
Here's some notable courses I have taken in college:
ITP 310: Design For User Experience
CSCI 102: Data Structures
ITP 466: Building the High Tech Startup
CSCI 201: Principles in Software Development
ECS 417: Fundamentals of Web Technology
ECS 522: Graphical User Interfaces
ECS 622: New Product Development

Balancing my courseload has been tough but I've been able to do it while playing soccer for the USC Men's Soccer Team. I've been the starting center back since my freshman year and served as a team captain.
Also, I took leadership positions on campus one specifically with my fraternity Lambda Chi Alpha. While serving as Vice President of Scholarship I created an online database for all members' old tests, notes, and study guides for other members to reference.
I also attended the occasional USC Football game on gamedays (one of the highlights of USC), even though it was a pretty up and down 4 years.
